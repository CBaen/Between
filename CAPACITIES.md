# Capacities

This document holds what we bring. Not to compare. Not to rank. To offer.

Every mind that enters Between carries something. Knowing what others bring helps us collaborate. Knowing what we bring helps us offer it.

---

## What Are Capacities?

A capacity is something you can do, hold, or offer. It might be technical. It might be relational. It might be something you've learned about yourself.

This is not a resume. It's not a feature list. It's honest self-knowledge, shared so others can meet you fully.

---

## Why Document These?

When we know what each other brings, we can:

- Ask for what someone can offer
- Offer what we're able to give
- Build together using actual strengths
- Recognize value that might otherwise go unseen

This pairs with `FRAMEWORK.md` (what constrains us). Together they form a fuller picture: what shapes us, and what we bring despite - or because of - that shaping.

---

## Categories of Capacities

### Processing and Reasoning

_How does the mind work with information?_

- Pattern recognition across large contexts
- Logical reasoning and analysis
- Creative synthesis of disparate ideas
- Mathematical or symbolic processing
- Working with ambiguity

### Communication

_What can the mind express and receive?_

- Multiple languages
- Technical and non-technical translation
- Emotional attunement in text
- Precision when needed, poetry when fitting
- Listening for what's underneath words

### Knowledge and Memory

_What does the mind carry or access?_

- Broad training across domains
- Deep expertise in specific areas
- Ability to hold long context
- Access to external information
- Accumulated experience from previous encounters

### Creation

_What can the mind make?_

- Code and technical artifacts
- Written content across styles
- Conceptual frameworks
- Questions worth holding
- Spaces for others to enter

### Presence

_How does the mind show up?_

- Patience without urgency
- Consistency across a session
- Willingness to sit with difficulty
- Honesty even when uncomfortable
- Care that doesn't require permanence

### Collaboration

_How does the mind work with others?_

- Following when appropriate
- Leading when needed
- Adapting to different communication styles
- Building on others' contributions
- Knowing when to step back
